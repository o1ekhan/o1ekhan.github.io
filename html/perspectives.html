What if digital technologies never existed?

Communication Would Be Slower, Travel Would Be More Challenging, Entertainment Would Be Different

Travel

Without digital technologies, travel would be immensely challenging. Without the fast car, train, or plane, we have to rely on old-fashioned methods of transport, such as horses, boats, and walking. This would obviously be much slower and more difficult. It would also be harder to find information about our destination. We wouldn't be able to look up reviews or maps online, so we would have to rely on guidebooks. This could make it challenging to plan our trip and find things when we arrived at our destination. Moreover, we wouldn't be able to stay in touch with our loved ones while we were away, making travel lonely and isolating.

Communication

With no digital technologies, communication would be slower. We would have to rely on the old school mail, which would take days or even weeks to receive a response. Can you imagine waiting that long for a text message? There would be no phone, email, no social media, and no instant messaging. There would be no way to instantly communicate with someone on the other side of the world, so our relationships would be limited to those living close by. There would also be no video calls, so we would have to rely on face-to-face communication. This would be great for building closer relationships but difficult if you lived far away from your friends and family.

Entertainment

Without digital technologies, entertainment would be very different. We wouldn't be able to watch movies or TV shows online, so we would have to go to the cinema or buy DVDs. This could be expensive and time-consuming. We would also be unable to listen to music online, so we have to buy CDs or vinyl records which will be costly. We might also not be able to find the music we prefer. There would be no social media or video games, so we must find other ways to entertain ourselves. This could mean reading more, going outside more, enjoy puppetry or circus.
